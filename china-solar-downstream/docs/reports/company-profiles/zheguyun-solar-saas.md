# Zheguyun (折股云) — Full-stack installer SaaS platform with freemium pivot

**Entrant Type:** Independent SaaS / digital infrastructure
**Primary Segment:** Installer workflow digitalization (design → construction → O&M)
**Founded:** Unknown
**Status:** Active (website confirmed operational as of 2026)

---

## Confidence Grade: ★2/★5

Confidence is low because Zheguyun has no public funding history, no disclosed revenue, no identifiable founding team, and no media coverage in English-language press. What is confirmed: an active product at zheguyun.com, product feature documentation published on CSDN (a Chinese developer community) in January–February 2024, and a verifiable free-tier launch in December 2023. These are functional proofs of existence and market engagement, not commercial proofs of scale. The company's opacity is typical for Chinese B2B SaaS firms at sub-Series A scale — they frequently operate with no investor relations function and minimal press outreach. The analytical conclusions drawn here are based on product architecture and launch timing rather than financial data.

---

## What Is Known

Zheguyun operates as a comprehensive SaaS platform targeting China's distributed solar installer base — estimated at 10,000+ small EPC firms as of 2024. The product stack covers the full installer workflow: AI-powered satellite map design tools (automating the preliminary site assessment phase), drone survey integration, PV yield simulation, electronic contract generation, warehouse and materials management, work-order tracking for construction teams, and an O&M module with real-time system monitoring and power generation analytics. ★3 (product features confirmed from CSDN documentation) The breadth of this stack is significant — most competitors address one or two phases of this workflow; Zheguyun's positioning is explicitly all-in-one.

The December 2023 free-tier launch is the most analytically significant confirmed data point. Prior to this, the platform operated on a paid model. The freemium pivot targets small installers who cannot justify SaaS subscription costs but represent the majority of China's installation volume by firm count. A distinctive feature of the platform is a B2C viral sharing toolkit — QR-code-based referral tools that allow installers to recruit neighboring farmer-homeowners as potential customers. This is not an O&M feature; it is a customer acquisition feature built into an O&M platform, suggesting Zheguyun understands that its installer customers' primary constraint is not system management but lead generation. ★3

An unusual enterprise-tier offering is 100% source-code delivery — larger installer clients can purchase the platform as white-labeled software rather than subscribing to a shared SaaS instance. This is rare in SaaS and suggests either limited confidence in the recurring revenue model or a pragmatic response to enterprise buyer preferences in a market where data sovereignty concerns are common. ★2 (noted in product documentation; rationale inferred)

## What Is Unknown and Why It Matters

The founding team, investment history, and any commercial scale metrics (active users, paying customers, ARR) are entirely unknown. This matters because the freemium transition could represent two very different conditions: (a) a well-funded startup deliberately investing in growth, absorbing free-tier costs to build a defensible user base before monetizing; or (b) a bootstrapped firm under competitive pressure that cut price to arrest churn. The analytical implication differs sharply between these cases. If (a), Zheguyun is executing a textbook platform playbook and may reach sufficient scale to become acquisition target for a panel OEM seeking digital distribution. If (b), the free tier may simply delay an inevitable exit. Without funding or revenue data, this distinction cannot be drawn.

---

## Analytical Significance

Zheguyun is included in this dataset as the clearest available signal of competitive dynamics in the installer SaaS segment. The December 2023 free-tier launch date is load-bearing: it follows the period when Solarman, Huawei FusionSolar, and inverter OEM monitoring apps had already captured the simple monitoring use case for free, and when Zhonglai's internal tools had become standard for large EPC firms. A new SaaS entrant launching free in late 2023 is implicitly conceding that the paid-monitoring space is crowded and staking a claim on workflow integration — the higher-value, stickier layer of the stack. The platform's existence confirms that China's installer digitalization market was not yet saturated as of 2024 and that independent SaaS vendors (not OEM-captive tools) still had a viable niche serving the long tail of small EPC firms.

## Korea Applicability

Zheguyun's model is directly applicable to the Korean installer market, which faces the same structural fragmentation: a large number of small EPC firms with limited digital capabilities. The freemium transition dynamics are particularly transferable — Korean SaaS vendors targeting solar installers should expect similar pressure to reduce price as monitoring becomes commoditized by inverter OEM apps (SMA, SolarEdge, Enphase all offer free monitoring). The differentiating layer — workflow integration and customer acquisition tools — is the same in both markets. The viral referral toolkit for farmer/rural homeowner recruitment has a near-direct analogue in Korea's rural solar expansion programs.
