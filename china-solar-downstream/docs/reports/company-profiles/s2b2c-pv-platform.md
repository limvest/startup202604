## Analytical Significance

光伏行家's analytical value lies entirely in its unverifiability. This is stated as the primary finding, not as a caveat. The intensive search effort — the same effort that confirmed Zheguyun's product stack, Chint Power's monitoring app, and Seeder Clean Energy's Crunchbase entry — found nothing for this entity or for any comparable standalone solar consumer marketplace in China. The structural interpretation of this null result is load-bearing for the report's broader thesis:

**No consumer solar marketplace achieved scale in China.** The S2B2C model wave of 2016–2018 produced no dominant platform. Instead, solar procurement was captured by: (a) vertically integrated incumbents whose captive supply chains made independent marketplaces unnecessary (Chint Anneng, Skyworth Solar); (b) general B2B procurement platforms (Alibaba 1688, JD Industrial) that absorbed solar components into their existing category structures; and (c) OEM-specific digital tools that locked installers into brand ecosystems. An independent, brand-agnostic consumer solar marketplace — the model that attracted venture capital in the US (EnergySage) and Germany (Enpal's early marketplace phase) — did not survive as a standalone business category in China.

The reasons are structural, not contingent. Chinese residential solar buyers in the rural household segment do not search for solar systems the way urban consumers search for electronics — the purchase is initiated by an installer or village intermediary, not by the consumer. The S2B2C model's consumer layer was theoretically elegant but practically irrelevant: rural homeowners do not comparison-shop solar systems online. The B layer (installer digitalization) was the durable value, and it was captured by vertically integrated OEM tools and, later, by independent SaaS platforms like Zheguyun. The S layer (component aggregation) was captured by general procurement platforms with broader SKU depth.

光伏行家's presence in the dataset — as an entity that cannot be found — is therefore retained deliberately. Its absence is evidence. It confirms the structural finding that the solar consumer marketplace category failed, not through competitive loss to a single winner, but through structural irrelevance in a market whose purchase dynamics did not support the model.

## Korea Applicability

The S2B2C failure in China is a direct cautionary input for the Korean market. Korea's distributed solar market has attracted interest in marketplace-style platforms — aggregators that connect homeowners with competing installers, analogous to EnergySage's US model. The Chinese experience suggests these platforms are structurally challenged when the purchase initiation pathway runs through installers or intermediaries rather than consumer-initiated search. In Korea's rural and agricultural solar market, the same dynamic applies: adoption is driven by village-level social networks, agricultural cooperative recommendations, and installer door-to-door outreach — not by online consumer comparison. A Korean solar marketplace aimed at this segment would face the same structural mismatch that apparently killed the S2B2C wave in China. Urban and commercial solar, where buyer sophistication is higher, may offer a more viable marketplace niche — but that is a different model targeting a different segment.
